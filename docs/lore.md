# Dungeon of Vim Lore

## World overview

Dungeon of Vim is a terminal-shaped prison where every wall, door, and line of
text remembers old habits. The world feels static now, but the room itself is a
teaching loop: you move, you read, and you make choices with intent.

The first block is small on purpose. New players learn the shape of the system
before the map stretches across more levels.

## Prison concept

You are not trapped by force alone. You are trapped by default actions and panic.
The cell is a test of discipline: one mistake can kill you, one good movement
opens your next thought.

## Vim mythology

Vim is the oldest spell in the prison.

To the inmates, Vim is not just a keybinding set — it is a way to:

- move with purpose, not guesswork
- separate movement from editing from command actions
- survive with a small set of reliable tools

You hear it in the walls because the prison itself was built to be navigated by
those who can think in verbs.

## tmux mythology

tmux is the art of many windows in one place.

Stories claim that tmux can split the prison into many panes, letting you
watch paths, whispers, and escape routes at the same time. In practical terms,
it is the power of keeping context while staying inside the same terminal.

## bash mythology

bash is the language of old machines.

For the newcomers, bash is the first doorway to scripting your own habits.
Where Vim changes the cursor, bash changes the day: it lets you name, move,
and combine actions as a short ritual.

## sudo mythology

sudo is the final blessing.

In this world, sudo is not instant power; it is earned authority.
When it arrives, the player does not become a god — they become the person who
can remove the last lock from the final gate.

## Why telnet is forbidden

Long ago, prisoners tried the old `telnet` route to bypass walls. It felt fast
and easy, but it leaked every secret in transit and taught no real skill.

In the lore, telnet is punished because it is insecure, obvious, and loud.
The prison listens.
